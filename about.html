Author ofFrom the Heart of Childhood

I do not think I found this work.
I think I gradually began to recognise something I could no longer ignore.

For nearly forty years I have worked closely with children, parents, teachers, and communities across Hong Kong and the UK.

I founded and led early childhood schools and spent decades observing how human beings grow, adapt, connect, and protect themselves.

But what shaped this work most deeply was not institutional leadership or professional training.

It was observation.

Watching what happens when a child feels truly safe, seen, and met.

And observing what happens when they do not.

Some children become very compliant.
Some withdraw.
Some become highly capable and attentive to everyone around them.

None of these responses are random.

They are intelligent adaptations in service of connection.

Over time, I began to recognise those same patterns in adults.

The overcarrying.
The constant managing.
The subtle exhaustion that can come from staying responsible for too much, for too long.

I came to understand that many of the ways we relate as adults begin much earlier than we realise.

Connected Presence grew slowly from this understanding.

From classrooms.
From conversations with parents and teachers.
From raising four children and now being a grandmother.
And from years of learning something both simple and profound:

Presence is not something we force.

It becomes more available when we feel safe enough to soften.

My work today brings together developmental understanding, deep listening, relational presence, and conscious facilitation.

I facilitate spaces where people can slow down, feel heard, and reconnect with the steadiness and wisdom already within them.

Not by becoming someone else.

But by reclaiming a more natural way of being with greater embodied awareness and presence.

The way we are met as children shapes how we relate as adults.

Background

A life of observing.

My work is informed by both lived experience and many years of study.

Alongside founding and leading early childhood schools, I trained as an Early Childhood Teacher and undertook further study in Therapeutic Play, Counselling, Nutrition, and Compassionate Inquiry.

I developed and led a UK government recognised training programme and continue to explore approaches that deepen understanding of human development, presence, and relationship.

I am also the author of From the Heart of Childhood: Reclaiming Presence for Connection.

These experiences have shaped my path, but the deepest learning has come through years of listening, observing, and being in relationship with children, families, and communities.

Ways to work with me

I offer spaces where Connected Presence can be experienced and practised in everyday life and relationship.

Some people come to this work through parenting.
Some through relationships, exhaustion, life transitions, or a longing to feel more connected to themselves.

At its heart, this work is about learning to listen more deeply — to ourselves, to others, and to the patterns that shape how we relate.

Connected Presence Circles

Small group spaces for listening, reflection, and shared presence.

Together we explore themes of relationships, parenting, childhood, human development, and the ways we learn to relate to ourselves and others.

These circles are open to all.

One-to-One Presence Sessions

Quiet spaces to slow down, feel heard, and explore the challenges, questions, or decisions arising in your life.

These sessions offer space for reflection, deep listening, and awareness to unfold naturally, helping you reconnect with your own clarity, steadiness, and inner knowing.
