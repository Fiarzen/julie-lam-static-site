Some children withdraw. Some become very compliant. Some become the helper, the peacemaker, the one who never makes a fuss. None of it is random — it is the beginning of a pattern we carry for life.

A quiet child is rarely *just* quiet. Quietness is a strategy, learned early and often rewarded. It says: *I have noticed that the room is calmer when I take up less space.*

> The adaptations that kept us safe as children become the habits that exhaust us as adults.

When we begin to see these patterns clearly — without shame, without rushing to fix them — something softens. We stop asking the child within us to keep performing, and we start asking what they actually needed all along.
