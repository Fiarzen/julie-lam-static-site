There is a tiredness that doesn't look like tiredness. It looks like capability. It looks like the person everyone relies on, the one who always has it handled, the one who would never dream of letting a thing drop.

We praise this. We promote it. We rarely ask what it costs.

Over-carrying is competence that has forgotten where its edges are. It is the slow erosion that happens when *being able to* quietly becomes *always having to*.

The invitation is not to become less capable. It is to notice the difference between carrying something because it is yours to carry, and carrying it because you never learned how to set it down.
