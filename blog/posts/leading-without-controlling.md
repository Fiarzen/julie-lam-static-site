True leadership does not control. It empowers through aware presence.

Control is seductive because it feels like responsibility. If I hold the reins tightly enough, nothing will go wrong. But control and trust cannot occupy the same room — and people can always feel which one is leading them.

> When we stop managing people and start being present to them, they begin to lead themselves.

What changes is subtle at first. Conversations slow down. Silences stop feeling like problems to solve. The people around us, no longer braced for correction, begin to bring more of themselves.

This is not a softer way to lead. It is a far more demanding one — because it asks us to be present rather than in charge.
